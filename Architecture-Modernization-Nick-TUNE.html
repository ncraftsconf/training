Workshop details

This is a 2-day workshop for business, product, UX, and technology professionals who want to learn about concepts that can help when modernizing old systems and ways of working, and to spend time discussing these concepts and sharing experiences with other professionals.

Workshop Topics

This workshop covers the following topics from the perspective of organizations that are trying to establish a product culture with empowered product teams (teams that are responsible for the entire value stream, from talking to customers to running their software in production).

  • Building the business case for modernization
  • Designing the business architecture
  • Identifying domains and subdomains
  • Organizing teams for value discovery and sustainable fast flow
  • Establishing an Architecture Modernization Enabling Team
  • Prioritizing and sequencing modernization work
  • Creating a learning culture and upskilling the organization

Workshop Style

This workshop is designed around a high-level of attendee participation. Much of the workshop content will be produced by attendees themselves, bringing their own personal experiences and perspectives on each topic.

This isn’t a workshop where the facilitators will be broadcasting most of the content from slides or providing highly specific solutions for every challenge. Modernization is a complex topic that touches on many factors and there isn’t a single right way or a single tool that can be applied in a given context.

This workshop will mostly involve the following types of activities: thinking and writing alone, working in small breakout sessions, and large group discussions.

Who Should Attend?

Anyone involved in building products and improving organizational performance in medium and large organizations would be able to contribute to the topics in this workshop. The most important criteria is that you want to improve your organization and are interested in an empowered product team approach

The following are some of the example roles that were in mind when this workshop was designed:

  • CTO, VP Eng, Engineering Manager
  • Senior / Principal / Staff Software Engineer
  • Head of Product, Product Manager / Owner
  • Service Designer, Business Analyst, UX / Product Designer
  • Agile Coach, Delivery Manager
  • Business / Enterprise / Software Architecture

About Nick TUNE

Nick is a Principal Consultant at Empathy Software who works with technology leaders to map strategy, architect systems, and build continuous delivery teams. He is the co-author of Architecture Modernization: Product, Domain, and Team-oriented (Leanpub) and Patterns, Principles and Practices of Domain-Driven Design (Wrox), and blogs monthly at ntcoding.co.uk/blog.
