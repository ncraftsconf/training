Workshop details

A loosely-coupled software architecture and an organisational structure to match is one of the biggest predictors of continuous delivery performance (Forsgren et al.). In this workshop you will explore the theory to understand the relationships between social and technical architectures, and you will apply hands-on techniques for modelling sociotechnical architectures including EventStorming, Domain-Driven Design, Team Topologies, and Dynamic Reteaming.

What you will learn:

  • The implications of Conway’s Law for creating loosely-coupled software systems and autonomous teams
  • The 5 principles of sociotechnical architecture: value-guided, domain-driven, socially-optimised, technical-pragmatic, CX-aware
  • How to use EventStorming to model domains
  • How to apply Domain-Driven Design to design a software system aligned with business goals
  • How to combine DDD and Team Topologies to optimise the design of teams for fast flow
  • How to choose sociotechnical relationship patterns - technical integration and team collaboration
  • How to evolve sociotechnical architectures using DDD, Team Topologies and Dynamic Reteaming
